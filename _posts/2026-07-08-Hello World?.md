## Hello... World?

I've always found the phrase *"Hello, World!"* a little strange.

Who exactly is saying hello here?

Is the computer introducing itself? Am I pretending to be the computer? Is the compiler just being polite?

For a programming exercise, it never really made much sense to me.

For a blog, though...

It feels oddly appropriate.

This is quite literally me saying **hello to the world**.

(Why do I spend so much time thinking about semantics? Your guess is as good as mine.)

## Why this blog exists

The main reason this blog exists is surprisingly selfish.

I'm writing for my future self.

He's a nice guy, but he has an absolutely terrible memory.

I'll spend hours understanding a concept, convince myself that I'll *definitely* remember it forever, and then six months later find myself reading the same documentation wondering why everything suddenly looks unfamiliar.

So this blog is my notebook.

A place where I can document things I've built, things I've broken, and—hopefully—things I've finally understood.

If someone else happens to find these posts useful, that's an added bonus.

## Learning by explaining

There's a quote often attributed to Richard Feynman that I really like:

> "If you want to master something, teach it."

Whether or not those were his exact words, the idea behind the **Feynman Technique** has stuck with me: if you can't explain something simply, you probably don't understand it as well as you think you do.

Writing forces me to slow down, question my assumptions, and fill in the gaps in my understanding.

Hopefully, it also makes the next person spend a little less time confused than I did.
